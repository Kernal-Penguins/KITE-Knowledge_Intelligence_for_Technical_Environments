LESSONS-LEARNED REPORT
Facility: Cedar Point Processing Facility
Report #: INC-2025-025
Date Issued: 2025-12-05
Prepared by: David Kim, Reliability
Equipment Involved: AC-12 (Air Compressor 12), FN-33 (Cooling Tower Fan 33)

Summary:
Two separate rotating equipment bearing failures occurred within a
three-month window in 2025 (AC-12 on 2025-06-11, FN-33 on 2025-08-22),
both traced to the same root cause: an unapproved grease substitution
(NLGI-1 in place of the specified NLGI-2 per SOP-AC-018) made during
PMs when the correct grease grade was out of stock in stores.

Pattern Identified:
Neither substitution was logged as a deviation or flagged for
follow-up re-lubrication at the time it occurred. Both failures
presented identically - bearing overheating with visible grease
breakdown/discoloration - despite occurring on different equipment
types (compressor vs. fan) in different areas of the plant. This
indicates a systemic gap in the substitution-approval process
described in SOP-AC-018, not an equipment-specific issue.

Contributing Factors:
1. Stores stockout of NLGI-2 grease was not flagged to planning before
   PMs were performed using substitute stock.
2. No supervisor approval step was actually enforced at the time,
   despite being documented policy.
3. No cross-equipment visibility - the AC-12 failure in June was not
   connected to the FN-33 failure in August until this review.

Recommendations:
1. Stores to alert maintenance planning proactively on any stockout of
   SOP-AC-018 specified lubricants, not just react to work order
   requests.
2. Supervisor approval for grease substitutions to be enforced via a
   sign-off field on the work order, not just written policy.
3. Reliability team to review all rotating equipment PMs performed
   during the stockout window for other undetected substitutions.

Reported per SOP-INC-001.
