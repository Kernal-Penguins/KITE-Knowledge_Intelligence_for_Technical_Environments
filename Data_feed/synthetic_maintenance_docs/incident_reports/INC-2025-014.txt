NEAR-MISS REPORT
Facility: Cedar Point Processing Facility
Report #: INC-2025-014
Date of Event: 2025-10-08
Reported by: Maria Torres
Equipment Involved: CB-15 (Conveyor Belt Drive 15)

Description:
During operation, CB-15 drive motor produced a loud grinding noise.
Operator on the platform nearby stepped back and stopped the conveyor
immediately via the local e-stop before any material jam or belt
failure occurred. No injury, no equipment damage beyond the bearing
itself.

Potential Consequence:
Had the bearing seized suddenly rather than giving audible warning, it
could have caused a belt derailment or motor failure with personnel
closer to the point of operation. The quick response by the operator
prevented escalation.

Root Cause Discussion:
Second bearing failure on this conveyor in six months (see
ML-CB15-2025-10-08). Lubrication PM had been skipped twice due to
production scheduling conflicts, extending the actual grease interval
well beyond the SOP-CB-005 standard.

Corrective Actions:
1. Bearing replaced same day (WO-10231).
2. Supervisor sign-off requirement added to the PM schedule to prevent
   further skips.
3. Recommend reviewing e-stop accessibility on this platform as a
   positive finding - operator response was effective.

Reported per SOP-INC-001.
