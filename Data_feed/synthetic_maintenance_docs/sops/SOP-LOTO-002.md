# SOP-LOTO-002: Lockout/Tagout Procedure

**Revision:** 6  |  **Effective Date:** 2018-07-01  |  **Owner:** EHS / Safety

## 1. Purpose
Defines the standard lockout/tagout process to ensure equipment is in
a zero-energy state before maintenance work begins.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all maintenance, repair, and inspection work on plant
equipment where unexpected energization or release of stored energy
could cause injury.

## 3. Procedure
1. Identify all energy sources for the equipment (electrical,
   pneumatic, hydraulic, gravity, stored pressure).
2. Notify affected personnel before shutdown.
3. Shut down equipment using normal operating controls.
4. Isolate all energy sources and apply locks/tags at each point.
5. Verify zero energy state (e.g., attempt to start, check pressure
   gauges at zero, verify no residual voltage).
6. Perform work.
7. Upon completion, remove tools, verify guards are reinstalled,
   remove locks/tags in reverse order, and notify affected personnel
   before restart.

## 4. Notes
This procedure is referenced by nearly all corrective maintenance work
orders in this facility and must be completed before any pump, valve,
conveyor, compressor, or generator work begins.
