# SOP-MP-014: Centrifugal Pump Mechanical Seal Replacement Procedure

**Revision:** 3  |  **Effective Date:** 2023-06-01  |  **Owner:** Maintenance Engineering

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the standard method for inspecting, removing, and
replacing mechanical seals on centrifugal pumps at Cedar Point Processing
Facility.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all centrifugal pumps with single mechanical seal
configuration, including but not limited to Unit 2 and Unit 3 process
water transfer pumps (e.g., P-104, P-207).

## 3. Prerequisites
- Pump isolated and locked out per SOP-LOTO-002
- Casing drained and depressurized
- Appropriate seal kit staged and verified against pump nameplate/spec

## 4. Procedure
1. Confirm zero energy state per SOP-LOTO-002 before proceeding.
2. Remove coupling guard and disconnect coupling.
3. Remove seal gland bolts and withdraw gland plate.
4. Inspect shaft sleeve for scoring or wear; replace if damaged.
5. Remove old seal faces and clean seal chamber.
6. Verify replacement seal material matches process fluid temperature
   and chemical compatibility rating on the pump datasheet. **Standard
   carbon/ceramic seals are rated to 220F; pumps operating above this
   temperature require silicon carbide (SiC/SiC) faces.**
7. Install new seal faces per manufacturer torque specification.
8. Reinstall gland plate, reconnect coupling, reinstall guard.
9. Remove lockout, restore pump to service, monitor for leaks during
   first 30 minutes of operation.
10. Log all work in the maintenance log system and close associated
    work order.

## 5. Notes
Seal failures presenting as glazing or scoring on the carbon face are
typically thermal in nature. If a pump has had more than one seal
failure with this symptom, verify actual operating temperature against
the seal's rated temperature before simply replacing in kind.
