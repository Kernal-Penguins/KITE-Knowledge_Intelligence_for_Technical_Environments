# SOP-CV-022: Control Valve Positioner Calibration and Inspection

**Revision:** 2  |  **Effective Date:** 2022-11-15  |  **Owner:** Instrumentation & Controls

## 1. Purpose
Defines the standard method for stroke testing, calibrating, and
visually inspecting pneumatic control valve positioners.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all pneumatically actuated control valves with positioners,
including outdoor-mounted units such as CV-220.

## 3. Frequency
Semi-annual, or following any reported control response failure.

## 4. Procedure
1. Notify operations before taking valve out of automatic control.
2. Perform full stroke test (0-100%) and record travel time and
   hysteresis.
3. Verify positioner output tracks input signal within 1% across the
   full range.
4. Visually inspect positioner enclosure, gasket, and cable glands for
   signs of wear, cracking, or water ingress.
5. For outdoor-mounted positioners, confirm enclosure rating is
   appropriate for the mounting location (minimum NEMA 4X recommended
   for direct rain exposure; NEMA 3R is not sufficient for this
   condition).
6. Record all findings on the standard inspection form, including a
   pass/fail result and next-due date.
7. Return valve to automatic control and confirm with operations.

## 5. Notes
Repeated positioner failures following rain events are a known failure
mode associated with inadequate enclosure ratings, not a calibration
issue. Do not simply recalibrate and close out - verify the physical
enclosure condition and rating first.
